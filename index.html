What is CoSolve

A methodology.
A platform.
One outcome: solutions.

Most organisations are skilled at identifying problems. What they lack is a structured, repeatable system for solving them — collaboratively, at the right level, with the right people in the room.

CoSolve is that system. It combines a battle-tested problem-solving methodology with a cloud platform where AI agents work alongside your team — structuring inputs, surfacing patterns, and keeping every case on track — from the moment a problem is named through to aligned, committed action.

1
Define the real problem
Structured frameworks move teams beyond symptoms to root causes — creating shared clarity on what actually needs to be solved before anyone proposes a solution.
2
Collaborate across the organisation
CoSolve brings cross-functional stakeholders into a single guided environment, ensuring no critical perspective is missing from the analysis.
3
Align on the best path forward
The platform guides teams from ideas to evaluated options, building genuine organisational commitment — not a top-down directive that gets quietly ignored.
4
Execute with accountability
Solutions are documented, owners assigned, and progress tracked — closing the gap between insight and implementation that kills most strategic initiatives.

Before the First Session

CoSolve is only as
good as what it
knows about you.

An AI system that reasons generically gives generic answers. CoSolve is built to reason on your organisation's specific context — your documents, your processes, your cases, your language. But that intelligence doesn't arrive pre-loaded. It is built, together, before your team opens the platform for the first time.

The CoSolve Foundry is that activation stage. It is a one-time engagement — separate from the annual subscription — covering everything required to get CoSolve reasoning on your real organisational knowledge from day one.

Without the Foundry, CoSolve can answer questions using general knowledge — useful, but not transformative. With the Foundry, it draws on your documented cases, your institutional knowledge, and your operational context. That is the difference between an AI assistant and an AI that understands your organisation.

01
Knowledge audit
We assess what documentation exists across your organisation — past cases, process documents, policies, and institutional knowledge. We identify what is available, what is useful, and how to structure it.
02
Document classification & upload
Relevant documents are classified, structured, and ingested into a dedicated knowledge base inside your own cloud environment. Nothing leaves your infrastructure during this process.
03
Platform deployment
ValueSims deploys CoSolve directly within your cloud environment alongside your team — configuring the AI agents to your organisational context, terminology, and priorities. A joint setup that your team owns from day one.
04
Validation & first session readiness
The platform is validated end-to-end before your first live session. AI agents are tested against real queries in your context. Your team is briefed and ready to run from the moment the Foundry concludes.
Foundry Outcome
A fully deployed, context-aware CoSolve environment — built on your knowledge, running in your infrastructure, ready for your first real session. Not a pilot. Not a sandbox. Your platform, live.
Commercial structure
The Foundry is priced as a one-time setup engagement — separate from and preceding the annual subscription. Contact us to discuss scope based on your organisation's documentation landscape.

About ValueSims

The company behind CoSolve

ValueSims is an Austrian technology and advisory company specialising in the design and delivery of structured problem-solving solutions for organisations navigating complex, cross-functional challenges. Founded and led by Vassilis Pangalis, ValueSims combines deep expertise in business methodology, digital simulation, and artificial intelligence to help organisations make better decisions — faster and with greater confidence.

CoSolve is ValueSims' flagship platform — the result of years working with organisations across multiple industries to understand why structured problem-solving succeeds when traditional approaches fail.
